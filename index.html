Your Team

Assemble Your TACT Team

Every sprint requires four roles. Each role has a distinct responsibility. All four must be confirmed and all members must be confident using AI before the sprint proceeds.

🎯
Sponsor
The Accountable Leader
Ensures the project succeeds. Monitors progress, removes blockers, and holds the team accountable. If tasks stall, the Sponsor pushes them through. Success or failure lands here.
⚙️
Super User
The Agent Boss
Owns the assistant end-to-end. Drives the build, coordinates SMEs and Change Agents, and is responsible for all downstream maintenance. This person is the bot's permanent home.
🧠
SMEs
The Validators
Confirm the logic makes sense during the build. After the build, they test, document what they like and dislike, and capture detailed findings for the Super User to act on in the Improve session.
🚀
Change Agents
The Champions
Test after SMEs and the Super User agree on release. Mirror SME testing from an end-user perspective. Own staff training and lead adoption across the broader team.

Before You Proceed

Two Reasons AI Projects Fail Before They Begin

These are not edge cases. They are the most common failure modes — and both happen before a single line of prompt is written.

🎯
No INTENT = No Build
A bot without direction doesn't fail slowly. It succeeds at solving the wrong problem — at full speed.

If your team cannot answer what problem you are solving, who it serves, what success looks like, and which steps belong to AI — you are not ready. Going into Week 1 without INTENT wastes everyone's time and produces a bot nobody uses.
🗑️
Garbage In = Garbage Out — at Scale
AI does not fix bad data or broken workflows. It replicates them — faster than any human ever could.

Unclean data produces confidently wrong outputs. A broken workflow redesigned around AI is still a broken workflow — now automated. Every error your current process makes, the bot will make — consistently, at volume, without apology.
These are the two things Week 0 exists to prevent.
🎲
Do Not Pass GO
In Monopoly, you don't collect $200 if you don't pass GO. Same rule here. All Week 0 deliverables must be complete and accessible before Week 1 is confirmed. A team that arrives at Week 1 without answers, without clean data, and without a documented workflow is not ready — and Week 1 does not happen.
⛔ INCOMPLETE WEEK 0 = DO NOT PROCEED
